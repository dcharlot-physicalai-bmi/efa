## VI-c. THE FLAGSHIP ‚Äî one structured energy, four jobs, on a body

The 2024‚Äì26 field split "learn a scalar energy" into three edges that never fuse (physics-EBMs conserve ¬∑ language-EBMs verify ¬∑ control-EBMs act); the center ‚Äî one energy doing all of it on a body, as its own safety certificate ‚Äî was empty. **This occupies it.** A single goal-conditioned `E(state, goal)`, structured on state-relative-to-goal, learned by fitted value iteration (no partition function), read four ways on a pendulum:

| job | reading of the SAME E | result |
|---|---|---|
| **control** | greedy descent on E | reaches goal **89%** of episodes (~33 steps) |
| **verify** | low E = valid action | picks helping over hurting action **76%** |
| **remember** | argmin over state | goal *is* the attractor (**0.155 rad**); many goals = many minima of one E |
| **certify** | E along trajectory | decreases **100%** of steps ‚Äî intrinsic Lyapunov certificate |

*Bounds: single simple body; verify weakest (76%, noisy oracle); control 89% (not all goals); approximate value; certify partly reflects greedy-descent construction but the 100% monotonicity confirms a valid control-Lyapunov function. The fusion the field left unbuilt ‚Äî the empty center of the physics‚Üîlanguage‚Üîcontrol triangle, occupied.*

**+ the LANGUAGE edge fused** (`ebm_lang.rs`): the goal is now a **discrete symbolic instruction** the energy must *decode* through a **learned embedding** ‚Äî not handed the goal coordinate. One `E(state, instruction)` over 6 commands: **REMEMBER ‚Äî each instruction's energy-minimum lands 0.069 rad (~4¬∞) from that command's goal ‚Üí the energy genuinely decoded the language**; CERTIFY 95%; VERIFY 75%; **CONTROL 61% (honest weak spot)**. The decoding is solved (0.069); greedy control degrades off-goal because the relative-to-goal structuring was deliberately withheld to make it genuinely language ‚Äî the same "better-shaped energy" lever, applied to the *path*. A symbolic command ‚Üí one structured energy ‚Üí verify/remember/certify on a body = the full physics‚Üîlanguage‚Üîcontrol centre.

**+ compositional language, *partial and honest*** (`ebm_compose2.rs`): command = **direction √ó distance**, two learned factor embeddings, trained on **4 of 6** combinations, tested on the **2 held-out**. Train: strong (0.077 rad, 98% control). Held-out: **one composed cleanly** (right¬∑far, +2.5: **0.231 rad, 100% control** ‚Äî the energy generalized to an unseen combination) but the **other failed** (left¬∑mid, ‚àí1.5: 1.1 rad, 3%). Compositionality is **present but fragile** at nano scale (1/2 held-out). Diagnostic, not random: the failed factor ("mid") appeared in training with only one direction, so composing it onto the other didn't transfer ‚Äî *emerging but incomplete disentanglement*. Next lever: disentangled/structured factor embeddings, more training, or scale. (Not dressed up as clean compositional language ‚Äî a real capability showing through, priced.)

**+ the right structure matters ‚Äî an informative negative** (`ebm_compose3.rs`): tried the obvious fix ‚Äî *structured additive composition* (decode factors ‚Üí goal vector `g = g_dir + g_dist` ‚Üí relative-to-goal energy). Train got tighter (0.037 rad, 99%) but **held-out got WORSE** (1.158 rad, 14%) than the free-embedding version (0.675, 52%). Diagnosis: the task's composition is **multiplicative** (goal = direction_sign √ó distance_magnitude), and additive 2-vector composition *structurally cannot represent a product* ‚Äî so the free nonlinear energy composed *better* than the wrongly-structured one. **Lesson (sharpens the thesis):** the lever is the *right* structure, not structure per se ‚Äî a mismatched inductive bias is worse than none; "structure beats scale" only when the structure matches the task's generative form. Correct next: composition matched to the task's multiplicative structure (nonlinear composition net, or trig-in-graph).

**+ the RIGHT (multiplicative) structure composes ‚Äî decoding gap collapses** (`ebm_compose4.rs`): matched the inductive bias to the task ‚Äî learn direction ‚Üí a *sign* scalar, distance ‚Üí a *magnitude* scalar, compose by **multiplication** (goal = sign √ó magnitude, an in-graph op), feed the offset Œ¥ = Œ∏ ‚àí goal to the energy. The factors disentangled cleanly (signs ‚àí0.93/+0.88; magnitudes 0.64/2.03/2.82, monotone), and the **train‚Üíheld-out decoding gap collapsed to 1.4√ó** (0.230 ‚Üí 0.317 rad) vs additive's 31√ó and free-embedding's 8.8√ó ‚Äî *both* unseen commands now get their goal placed (0.263, 0.371 rad). That is compositional generalization, on the decode/remember axis, achieved by the right structure. **Priced honestly:** control is weak (held-out 27%; even one train combo 70%) ‚Äî feeding raw Œ¥ buys compositional decoding but makes a poorly-shaped *control* landscape (worse than compose2's relative-feature 52%). So the two needs separated cleanly: **multiplicative composition solves compositional *decoding*; strong *control* needs the relative cos/sin features** ‚Äî marrying them requires cos/sin of the composed angle in Ferric's autograd (not yet present). Net across the thread: compositional decoding is now *solved by the right structure*; compositional *control* is a bounded engineering step (add trig ops), not a mystery.

**+ trig ops added to Ferric; the "marriage" is an honest negative that reveals a tension** (`ebm_compose5.rs`, + `sin`/`cos` now in Ferric's `Tensor` and autograd, gradient-verified sin‚Ä≤=cos / cos‚Ä≤=‚àísin). Marrying the two right structures ‚Äî compose gŒ∏ = a¬∑b *multiplicatively* (decode) then build cos(Œ∏‚àígŒ∏), sin(Œ∏‚àígŒ∏) *in-graph* for control ‚Äî restored **sharp train decoding** (0.086 rad) and clean control where factors land right (two combos 100%), but **held-out did NOT improve** (0.692 rad, 26%). Root cause, diagnosed: the relative feature cos(Œ∏‚àígŒ∏) is *tolerant of goal-placement error* (a 0.1-rad gŒ∏ error barely moves the energy), so FVI never tightly pins the compositional factors ‚Äî they drift ~0.1‚Äì0.15 (visible: train products land at ‚àí0.60/1.37 vs ‚àí0.5/1.5) and that drift compounds in held-out products. compose4's raw-Œ¥ pinned factors better (0.317) but gave a bad *control* landscape. **The two structures pull against each other in one shared energy:** decode-pinning wants goal error penalized directly (raw Œ¥); control-shaping wants the tolerant relative angle. So single-energy compositional *control* is a genuine representation-tension problem, not a missing-op one ‚Äî the trig ops work perfectly (they're a permanent Ferric gain). Clean next: pin the factors explicitly (auxiliary decode term, or a two-stage decode‚Üícontrol) ‚Äî legitimate, since instruction‚Üígoal is arguably a separate module from goal‚Üíaction. **Honest thread verdict: compositional *decoding* solved by the right structure; compositional *control* remains open at nano, now with a diagnosed mechanism (goal-error tolerance vs factor-pinning) rather than a missing capability.**

**+ decisive splitter ‚Äî placement was the whole problem, and a WALK-BACK of the tolerance claim** (`ebm_compose6.rs`; designed by an adversarial multi-agent pass that found the move I'd missed). Froze the factors at their TRUE values (a=[‚àí1,1], b=[0.5,1.5,2.5]) and re-ran FVI. **Result: both held-out commands PASS ‚Äî left¬∑mid 0.228 rad / 100% control, right¬∑far 0.223 rad / 100% control.** So control *transfers* with correct factors ‚áí **placement (factor values) was the whole problem; the energy shape carries control fine** (5/6 commands at 100%, including both held-out; one *training* command, right¬∑near/goal 0.5, sits at 27% ‚Äî a residual-MLP control quirk near the low-torque goal, unrelated to composition). **WALK-BACK (correction under test):** compose5 claimed the relative feature is *tolerant of goal error ‚Üí FVI has no incentive to pin the factors.* The tolerance probe **falsifies the strong form** ‚Äî MSE(truth)=0.00055 **<** MSE(drifted)=0.00061, so truth is genuinely the *lower-loss* solution and the incentive to pin **exists**. Not a flat landscape ‚Äî an under-driven one. **Consequence:** a direct decode-pin (two-stage grounding, or an auxiliary factor-pin loss on the 4 TRAIN commands only) should close compositional control cleanly ‚Äî it supplies the steeper signal joint FVI lacked, and truth is already the better fit. The heavier structural-well reshape is *not* required. Compositional control is now a solved-in-principle, one-experiment-away result, with the mechanism corrected honestly.

**+ COMPOSITIONAL CONTROL CLOSED ‚Äî two-stage grounding** (`ebm_compose7.rs`): Stage A learns the factors a(dir),b(dist) by regressing a¬∑b to the true TRAIN goals only, **from NEUTRAL init (b=[1,1,1])**; Stage B freezes them and runs the relative-feature control FVI. **Result: both held-out commands PASS ‚Äî left¬∑mid 0.228 rad / 100% control, right¬∑far 0.223 rad / 100% control** (held-out control 26% ‚Üí **100%**). Thread complete: the compositional-language arc (free 1/2 ‚Üí additive worse ‚Üí multiplicative decodes ‚Üí splitter: placement was the block ‚Üí two-stage grounding) **closes compositional control**, with the right structure at each step.

### VII ‚Äî Ingesting SOTA into Rust (the "don't be rigid" program)

Mandate: absorb the frontier into EFA's own Rust rather than defend turf. Roadmap: [INGEST-ROADMAP.md](INGEST-ROADMAP.md). Two thesis revisions adopted: (1) the inference primitive becomes multi-step continuous energy **descent** (was 1-step discrete argmin ‚Äî never actually descent); (2) "one energy" ‚Üí "one shared latent + a coordinated family of energies."

**Step 1 Phase-0 ‚Äî INGEST EBT-Policy, honest negative** (`ebm_actdescent.rs`): put the pendulum dynamics in-graph and descended the FVI *value* energy over a continuous torque via `grad()`, K‚àà{1,2,4,8,16}. **Result: continuous descent STALLS (1‚Äì3% reach) vs the discrete-greedy baseline's 100%** ‚Äî actions are ultra-smooth (|Œîu|‚âà0.01‚Äì0.13) but lack authority. Diagnosis: a single small timestep gives ‚àÇ(next-state)/‚àÇu ‚àù DT=0.05, so ‚àÇV/‚àÇu is tiny and descent under-steps; discrete argmin dodges this by *selecting* a full-magnitude torque. **The de-risk spike worked exactly as intended** ‚Äî it proves you can't naively descend a 1-step value over continuous actions; EBT-Policy's edge requires a *dedicated action-energy* Q(s,u,g) with u entering directly (Phase 1), or training *through* the descent. Redirects to Phase 1; recorded, not buried.

**Step 1 Phase-1 ‚Äî dedicated action-energy, ALSO an honest negative** (`ebm_actdescent2.rs`): put the torque *directly* in the energy input (columns u, u¬≤) and FVI-trained Q(s,u,g) with the action sampled continuously. **Still stalls (0‚Äì4% reach)** with near-zero actions. The cause is now *fundamental*, not a leverage tuning issue: **any one-step-lookahead energy is nearly flat in the action** ‚Äî over a single DT=0.05 step the torque barely moves the immediate cost or next-state value (both u-channels ‚àù DT), so ‚àÇQ/‚àÇu is tiny whether u enters through the dynamics (Phase 0) or directly (Phase 1). Discrete argmin dodges this by *ranking* 5 full-magnitude actions; gradient descent scales its step by the tiny gradient and stalls. **Lesson: EBT-Policy's edge is not "descend any energy" ‚Äî it is an energy SHAPED for descent (low at good actions), which requires training the energy *for* descent, not a Bellman value.**
