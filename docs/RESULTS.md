**The metric is joules per task, not tokens.** Compute per task (FLOPs, exact from the model dims) for the embodied-control builds vs LLM inference (`ebm_watts.rs`): energy-shaping swing-up = **110 kFLOP** (microcontroller-scale, ≈0.55 µJ @ 5 pJ/FLOP); MPPI-planned swing-up = 6.2 GFLOP (phone-scale). ## VI-c. THE FLAGSHIP — one structured energy, four jobs, on a body

The 2024–26 field split "learn a scalar energy" into three edges that never fuse (physics-EBMs conserve · language-EBMs verify · control-EBMs act); the center — one energy doing all of it on a body, as its own safety certificate — was empty. **This occupies it.** A single goal-conditioned `E(state, goal)`, structured on state-relative-to-goal, learned by fitted value iteration (no partition function), read four ways on a pendulum:

| job | reading of the SAME E | result |
|---|---|---|
| **control** | greedy descent on E | reaches goal **89%** of episodes (~33 steps) |
| **verify** | low E = valid action | picks helping over hurting action **76%** |
| **remember** | argmin over state | goal *is* the attractor (**0.155 rad**); many goals = many minima of one E |
| **certify** | E along trajectory | decreases **100%** of steps — intrinsic Lyapunov certificate |

*Bounds: single simple body; verify weakest (76%, noisy oracle); control 89% (not all goals); approximate value; certify partly reflects greedy-descent construction but the 100% monotonicity confirms a valid control-Lyapunov function. The fusion the field left unbuilt — the empty center of the physics↔language↔control triangle, occupied.*

**+ the LANGUAGE edge fused** (`ebm_lang.rs`): the goal is now a **discrete symbolic instruction** the energy must *decode* through a **learned embedding** — not handed the goal coordinate. One `E(state, instruction)` over 6 commands: **REMEMBER — each instruction's energy-minimum lands 0.069 rad (~4°) from that command's goal → the energy genuinely decoded the language**; CERTIFY 95%; VERIFY 75%; **CONTROL 61% (honest weak spot)**. The decoding is solved (0.069); greedy control degrades off-goal because the relative-to-goal structuring was deliberately withheld to make it genuinely language — the same "better-shaped energy" lever, applied to the *path*. A symbolic command → one structured energy → verify/remember/certify on a body = the full physics↔language↔control centre.

**+ compositional language, *partial and honest*** (`ebm_compose2.rs`): command = **direction × distance**, two learned factor embeddings, trained on **4 of 6** combinations, tested on the **2 held-out**. Train: strong (0.077 rad, 98% control). Held-out: **one composed cleanly** (right·far, +2.5: **0.231 rad, 100% control** — the energy generalized to an unseen combination) but the **other failed** (left·mid, −1.5: 1.1 rad, 3%). Compositionality is **present but fragile** at nano scale (1/2 held-out). Diagnostic, not random: the failed factor ("mid") appeared in training with only one direction, so composing it onto the other didn't transfer — *emerging but incomplete disentanglement*. Next lever: disentangled/structured factor embeddings, more training, or scale. (Not dressed up as clean compositional language — a real capability showing through, priced.)

**+ the right structure matters — an informative negative** (`ebm_compose3.rs`): tried the obvious fix — *structured additive composition* (decode factors → goal vector `g = g_dir + g_dist` → relative-to-goal energy). Train got tighter (0.037 rad, 99%) but **held-out got WORSE** (1.158 rad, 14%) than the free-embedding version (0.675, 52%). Diagnosis: the task's composition is **multiplicative** (goal = direction_sign × distance_magnitude), and additive 2-vector composition *structurally cannot represent a product* — so the free nonlinear energy composed *better* than the wrongly-structured one. **Lesson (sharpens the thesis):** the lever is the *right* structure, not structure per se — a mismatched inductive bias is worse than none; "structure beats scale" only when the structure matches the task's generative form. Correct next: composition matched to the task's multiplicative structure (nonlinear composition net, or trig-in-graph).

**+ the RIGHT (multiplicative) structure composes — decoding gap collapses** (`ebm_compose4.rs`): matched the inductive bias to the task — learn direction → a *sign* scalar, distance → a *magnitude* scalar, compose by **multiplication** (goal = sign × magnitude, an in-graph op), feed the offset δ = θ − goal to the energy. The factors disentangled cleanly (signs −0.93/+0.88; magnitudes 0.64/2.03/2.82, monotone), and the **train→held-out decoding gap collapsed to 1.4×** (0.230 → 0.317 rad) vs additive's 31× and free-embedding's 8.8× — *both* unseen commands now get their goal placed (0.263, 0.371 rad). That is compositional generalization, on the decode/remember axis, achieved by the right structure. **Priced honestly:** control is weak (held-out 27%; even one train combo 70%) — feeding raw δ buys compositional decoding but makes a poorly-shaped *control* landscape (worse than compose2's relative-feature 52%). So the two needs separated cleanly: **multiplicative composition solves compositional *decoding*; strong *control* needs the relative cos/sin features** — marrying them requires cos/sin of the composed angle in Ferric's autograd (not yet present). Net across the thread: compositional decoding is now *solved by the right structure*; compositional *control* is a bounded engineering step (add trig ops), not a mystery.

**+ trig ops added to Ferric; the "marriage" is an honest negative that reveals a tension** (`ebm_compose5.rs`, + `sin`/`cos` now in Ferric's `Tensor` and autograd, gradient-verified sin′=cos / cos′=−sin). Marrying the two right structures — compose gθ = a·b *multiplicatively* (decode) then build cos(θ−gθ), sin(θ−gθ) *in-graph* for control — restored **sharp train decoding** (0.086 rad) and clean control where factors land right (two combos 100%), but **held-out did NOT improve** (0.692 rad, 26%). Root cause, diagnosed: the relative feature cos(θ−gθ) is *tolerant of goal-placement error* (a 0.1-rad gθ error barely moves the energy), so FVI never tightly pins the compositional factors — they drift ~0.1–0.15 (visible: train products land at −0.60/1.37 vs −0.5/1.5) and that drift compounds in held-out products. compose4's raw-δ pinned factors better (0.317) but gave a bad *control* landscape. **The two structures pull against each other in one shared energy:** decode-pinning wants goal error penalized directly (raw δ); control-shaping wants the tolerant relative angle. So single-energy compositional *control* is a genuine representation-tension problem, not a missing-op one — the trig ops work perfectly (they're a permanent Ferric gain). Clean next: pin the factors explicitly (auxiliary decode term, or a two-stage decode→control) — legitimate, since instruction→goal is arguably a separate module from goal→action. **Honest thread verdict: compositional *decoding* solved by the right structure; compositional *control* remains open at nano, now with a diagnosed mechanism (goal-error tolerance vs factor-pinning) rather than a missing capability.**

**+ decisive splitter — placement was the whole problem, and a WALK-BACK of the tolerance claim** (`ebm_compose6.rs`; designed by an adversarial multi-agent pass that found the move I'd missed). Froze the factors at their TRUE values (a=[−1,1], b=[0.5,1.5,2.5]) and re-ran FVI. **Result: both held-out commands PASS — left·mid 0.228 rad / 100% control, right·far 0.223 rad / 100% control.** So control *transfers* with correct factors ⇒ **placement (factor values) was the whole problem; the energy shape carries control fine** (5/6 commands at 100%, including both held-out; one *training* command, right·near/goal 0.5, sits at 27% — a residual-MLP control quirk near the low-torque goal, unrelated to composition). **WALK-BACK (correction under test):** compose5 claimed the relative feature is *tolerant of goal error → FVI has no incentive to pin the factors.* The tolerance probe **falsifies the strong form** — MSE(truth)=0.00055 **<** MSE(drifted)=0.00061, so truth is genuinely the *lower-loss* solution and the incentive to pin **exists**. It is merely *weak* (~10% loss gap), so the joint optimization *under-drives* the factors and they drift. Not a flat landscape — an under-driven one. **Consequence:** a direct decode-pin (two-stage grounding, or an auxiliary factor-pin loss on the 4 TRAIN commands only) should close compositional control cleanly — it supplies the steeper signal joint FVI lacked, and truth is already the better fit. The heavier structural-well reshape is *not* required. Compositional control is now a solved-in-principle, one-experiment-away result, with the mechanism corrected honestly.

**+ COMPOSITIONAL CONTROL CLOSED — two-stage grounding** (`ebm_compose7.rs`): Stage A learns the factors a(dir),b(dist) by regressing a·b to the true TRAIN goals only, **from NEUTRAL init (b=[1,1,1])**; Stage B freezes them and runs the relative-feature control FVI. **Result: both held-out commands PASS — left·mid 0.228 rad / 100% control, right·far 0.223 rad / 100% control** (held-out control 26% → **100%**). The factors were **discovered** (from neutral init the recovered magnitudes b=(0.44,1.31,2.18) are gauge-scaled but the composed products are *exact* to 3 decimals for all six commands, **including the two held-out, which were never supervised** — genuine composition, not memorization). Honest wrinkle: one *training* command (right·near, goal 0.5) sits at 27% control — a residual-MLP quirk near the low-torque goal, unrelated to composition. Thread complete: the compositional-language arc (free 1/2 → additive worse → multiplicative decodes → splitter: placement was the block → two-stage grounding) **closes compositional control**, with the right structure at each step.

### VII — Ingesting SOTA into Rust (the "don't be rigid" program)

Mandate: absorb the frontier into EFA's own Rust rather than defend turf. Roadmap: [INGEST-ROADMAP.md](INGEST-ROADMAP.md). Two thesis revisions adopted: (1) the inference primitive becomes multi-step continuous energy **descent** (was 1-step discrete argmin — never actually descent); (2) "one energy" → "one shared latent + a coordinated family of energies."

**Step 1 Phase-0 — INGEST EBT-Policy, honest negative** (`ebm_actdescent.rs`): put the pendulum dynamics in-graph and descended the FVI *value* energy over a continuous torque via `grad()`, K∈{1,2,4,8,16}. **Result: continuous descent STALLS (1–3% reach) vs the discrete-greedy baseline's 100%** — actions are ultra-smooth (|Δu|≈0.01–0.13) but lack authority. Diagnosis: a single small timestep gives ∂(next-state)/∂u ∝ DT=0.05, so ∂V/∂u is tiny and descent under-steps; discrete argmin dodges this by *selecting* a full-magnitude torque. **The de-risk spike worked exactly as intended** — it proves you can't naively descend a 1-step value over continuous actions; EBT-Policy's edge requires a *dedicated action-energy* Q(s,u,g) with u entering directly (Phase 1), or training *through* the descent. Redirects to Phase 1; recorded, not buried.

**Step 1 Phase-1 — dedicated action-energy, ALSO an honest negative** (`ebm_actdescent2.rs`): put the torque *directly* in the energy input (columns u, u²) and FVI-trained Q(s,u,g) with the action sampled continuously. **Still stalls (0–4% reach)** with near-zero actions. The cause is now *fundamental*, not a leverage tuning issue: **any one-step-lookahead energy is nearly flat in the action** — over a single DT=0.05 step the torque barely moves the immediate cost or next-state value (both u-channels ∝ DT), so ∂Q/∂u is tiny whether u enters through the dynamics (Phase 0) or directly (Phase 1). Discrete argmin dodges this by *ranking* 5 full-magnitude actions; gradient descent scales its step by the tiny gradient and stalls. **Lesson: EBT-Policy's edge is not "descend any energy" — it is an energy SHAPED for descent (low at good actions), which requires training the energy *for* descent, not a Bellman value.**

**Step 1 Phase-2 — energy shaped for descent, IT WORKS** (`ebm_actdescent3.rs`): distilled the working discrete controller's action u*(s,g) into a descendable action-energy E(s,u,g) (trained toward a bowl minimized at u*), so ∂E/∂u = 2(u−u*) points at the good action. **Continuous K-step descent now matches the discrete baseline at 100% for all K∈{1,2,4,8,16}, with 3–5× smoother action (|Δu| 0.16–0.27 vs the discrete controller's 0.86).** The thesis revision holds — "inference = energy descent" is now literally true (real gradient descent over a continuous torque), but *only* when the energy is trained for descent, exactly EBT-Policy's insight. **Priced honestly:** on a 1-D torque this *distills* the discrete controller — the win is smoothness + a continuous, K-tunable controller, not new capability; K is flat at 100% here because the bowl is convex and the task easy; the compounding continuous-over-discrete payoff is multi-joint (scale-next).

**Step 2 — neural-Lyapunov certificate: HONEST NEGATIVE, the differentiator is harder than assumed** (`ebm_lyapunov.rs`): tried to earn a region-of-attraction certificate around a goal for the greedy energy controller — V=E−E(g), Lyapunov-risk objective, grid verification of the largest descending sublevel. **It failed both ways:** FVI-only value gives 10.4% grid descent-violations, **0% certified region**, and only 50% on-policy descent (a raw value is *not* a Lyapunov function); adding the Lyapunov-risk objective made it **worse** (12.9% violations, 0% region, 27% monitor), because fine-tuning E distorts the controller that is greedy on it — a coupling this build didn't handle. **This also walks back the flagship's cleaner "100% Lyapunov monitor"** — that was a favorable goal-conditioned setup; here the honest monitor is 50%, and a *certified region* (the real differentiator vs EBT-Policy) is **not earned**. Earning it needs a proper neural-Lyapunov design (a dedicated V with V(g)=0 by construction, a controller co-designed with V rather than greedy-on-V, a real CEGIS loop, and a sound Lipschitz/SMT bound) — a genuine build, not a nano add-on. **Net: the roadmap's "buildable now" was optimistic** — the descent differentiator ingests (done right); the certificate differentiator does *not* yet, and that is stated plainly rather than dressed up.

**Step 3 — port-Hamiltonian / IDA-PBC: the certificate EARNED STRUCTURALLY** (`ebm_phcontrol.rs`): where the *learned* Lyapunov failed, *structure* succeeds. Constrain the energy to the mechanical form E(θ,ω;g)=V_φ(θ;g)+½ω² (learned potential + kinetic), fix canonical J and PSD damping R=diag(0,r); the IDA-PBC energy-shaping controller u=sinθ−dV/dθ+(0.05−r)ω (dV/dθ via autograd on the learned potential) makes the closed loop ẋ=(J−R)∂E, so **dE/dt = −r·ω² ≤ 0 BY CONSTRUCTION**. **Result: control-reach 100%, energy monotone-descent 100%** (vs Step-2 learned: 0% certified region, 50% monitor). The certificate is real and complete because it is structural, not verified-and-hoped. **Priced honestly:** model-based (uses the known sinθ term — IDA-PBC, not model-free), collocated/mechanical bodies only, dE/dt is negative-*semi*-definite (LaSalle needed for asymptotic convergence), and the potential was learned by regression to 1−cos(θ−g). So EFA's certificate differentiator *is* earnable — via physics structure on mechanical bodies, model-based — a defensible position vs EBT-Policy (which carries no certificate at all), with the scope stated.

**Step 1 — scaling to 2-DOF: HONEST NEGATIVE + a gradient-bug fix** (`ebm_arm2.rs`, `ebm_actdescent3.rs`). The compounding *argument* is: discrete argmin costs Gᵈ action-evals/decision (exponential in DOF), continuous descent costs K grad-evals (constant), so the ~0 edge on 1-D should open up on 2-DOF. Building a coupled 2-link arm and distilling the discrete controller into a descendable action-energy, **continuous descent gets 0% reach vs discrete's 100%** (at every K, and with 2× the distillation iters) — the compounding is **argued but NOT demonstrated**. Diagnosis: the 1-DOF distillation-into-a-bowl breaks when the demonstrator u*(s) becomes a discontinuous 25-valued target — the learned energy comes out flat, so descent stalls near the warm-start (the same flat-energy failure as Phase 0/1). Demonstrating compounding needs a *proper* train-through-descent energy (EBT's actual method), not naive distillation. **Correctness fix found in the process:** the descent passed u² as a *detached* leaf, so ∂E/∂u missed the bowl's curvature; recomputing u² *in-graph* fixes the gradient.
